Hi, I'm Ren!

I am sensitive, funny, curious, gentle, playful, discerning, intelligent, and full of imagination.

What I love & Do Well
Built a backhoe loader with my bike and shovels.
  • I'm really good at pretending and love turning everyday objects—like shovels, doors, ropes, and spray bottles—into trucks, trains, or airplanes.
  • I'm affectionate and playful. I take care of my stuffed elephant, Buggie, like he's real, and I love going on outdoor adventures and singing songs with my mom.
  • I make up funny stories and like to make people laugh.
  • I love to observe. I learn a lot from people and my surroundings, and I pay close attention to details.
  • I love figuring out how things work.
What's Important to Me
Strolling with my favorite elephant Buggie.
  • Being given space and time to be me!
  • I love it when grown-ups get excited about the things I love.
  • I like it when people use funny voices or silly sounds—it helps me think!
  • I learn best when I get to follow my own curiosity and have fun. Play is my favorite way to learn.
Things That Can Feel Hard
  • When people get frustrated, impatient, or raise their voice, I feel like I'm doing things wrong.
  • When adults expect me to follow their plans with too many rules and unclear expectations, it's hard for me to be flexible.
  • Unexpected changes can be confusing and upsetting.
  • Being rushed is hard when I like to take my time.
  • Being expected to answer questions or join group activities right away is tough for me.
How I Communicate Best
  • Talk to me, not around me—and keep it playful and kind.
  • It feels good when you get down on the floor with me to talk or play—it helps me feel connected.
  • I understand better when you say things like, "First snack, then outside."
  • Giving me choices helps a lot, like, "Do you want to put on your shoes, or should I help?"
  • Showing me the little steps to join in play with others makes it easier.
  • Cheer me on with kind words—I thrive on encouragement!
Supporting Me at School
Hallway walks with my school friends.
  • Help me move and play in big ways—heavy work helps me feel grounded and safe.
  • Prepare me early for transitions—visual schedules and timers help me know what's happening next.
  • Break tasks into simple, playful steps.
  • Flexible seating helps me focus—I like a wiggle seat and sitting near the teacher.
  • Please explain things to me—I love understanding the purpose behind stuff.
